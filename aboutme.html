About Me

My name is James Stevick and I am a hardware engineer at Clarity Movement, a hardware startup tackling global air pollution with low-cost air quality monitoring.

After attending the infamous yet inspirational Berkeley High School, I studied economics and physics at Claremont McKenna College, unable to make up my mind on a career path. Turns out you can combine the two, so I did just that, studying Mechanical Engineering at University of Michigan. I fell in love with tinkering and hacking which naturally pushed me toward the adult sandbox of sensors and mechatronics.

At Clarity, I am a multidisciplinary engineer who loves hands-on prototyping, research, and development. I design experiments and test setups for data-driven development and optimization of our hardware and sensors.

In my spare time I like to rock-climb, bike, cook, spend time in my ceramics studio, and all things outdoors. I also nerd out over my desktop CNC / Laser Engraver / 3D Printer. However, my basic needs in life are french fries, beer, and a hot-tub.

Reach out to me any time - I'd love to chat!
